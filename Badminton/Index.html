The Racket

A badminton racket is made of lightweight materials such as graphite, carbon fiber, or aluminum. The racket head is strung with synthetic gut or other materials. The overall weight of the racket typically ranges from 85 to 100 grams.

The Shuttlecock

A badminton shuttlecock, also known as a birdie, is a cone-shaped piece of rubber or plastic with feathers attached to the wide end. The shuttlecock is designed to travel in a straight path when hit with a racket. The feathers are typically made from goose or duck feathers.

The Court

A badminton court is 44 feet long and 20 feet wide for singles matches and 44 feet long and 22 feet wide for doubles matches. The court is divided into two halves by a net that stands 5 feet high.
